GIRAFFE

The giraffe is a large African hoofed mammal belonging to the genus Giraffa. It is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant on Earth. Traditionally, giraffes have been thought of as one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

Giraffes are found in various parts of sub-Saharan Africa, particularly in East and Southern Africa. They typically inhabit savannahs and woodlands, where they can find food and water. Specific locations where you might see them include national parks like Serengeti National Park in Tanzania, Amboseli National Park in Kenya, and Kruger National Park in South Africa
